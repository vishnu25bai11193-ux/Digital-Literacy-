Phishing is a deceptive form of cyberattack where perpetrators disguise themselves as trustworthy entities to steal sensitive information. Typically delivered via email, SMS, or phone, these messages create a false sense of urgency or fear, such as claiming an account is compromised or a payment is overdue. The goal is to trick users into clicking malicious links or downloading attachments that install malware or lead to fake login pages designed to harvest credentials, credit card numbers, and personal data.
Modern phishing has evolved beyond generic spam into sophisticated, targeted tactics like spear phishing, which uses personalized details to increase credibility. Because it exploits human psychology rather than just technical loopholes, it remains one of the most effective entry points for data breaches. Robust defense requires a combination of multi-factor authentication (MFA), advanced email filtering, and consistent user education to recognize suspicious red flags
Preventative Measures
A robust defense requires a multi-layered approach that combines technical controls with active user vigilance: 
MFA (Multi-Factor Authentication): Implementing phishing-resistant MFA, such as FIDO2 or hardware security keys, is critical. Standard SMS or app-based codes are now regularly bypassed by modern phishing kits.
Email Authentication Protocols: Use SPF, DKIM, and DMARC to verify sender identity. This prevents attackers from "spoofing" your domain to send fake emails to employees or customers.
Continuous Training: Regular phishing simulations and awareness campaigns help build a "human firewall." Studies show that 12 months of training can reduce employee susceptibility from over 30% to just 4%.
Advanced Filtering: Deploy AI-powered email security that can detect anomalies in language or behavior, catching sophisticated "spear phishing" attempts that bypass traditional spam filters.

Real-World Examples
Recent high-profile incidents demonstrate that no organization—regardless of size or technical expertise—is immune:
Booking.com (2024–2025): Attackers impersonated the platform to target hotels with a "ClickFix" trick, prompting staff to run malicious commands that stole payment and credential data.
Change Healthcare (2024): A massive phishing-led breach exposed the medical data of approximately 100 million users, severely disrupting billing and pharmacy services across the United States.
MGM Resorts (2023): A group known as "Scattered Spider" used voice phishing (vishing) to compromise credentials, leading to a massive operational shutdown of their hotel and casino systems.
Colonial Pipeline (2021): A single compromised password, likely obtained through phishing, allowed hackers to launch a ransomware attack that shut down half of the U.S. East Coast's fuel supply for a week.
Google and Facebook ($100M Scam): Between 2013 and 2015, a lone attacker used fake invoices to trick employees at both tech giants into wiring over $100 million to fraudulent accounts.
